📖 Reference & Compliance

Glossary of WFM / ECP Terms

10 terms
Erlang CREFERENCE
Forecasting & Staffing Math

The queuing-theory formula used to calculate how many agents are needed to hit a target service level given call volume, average handle time, and an acceptable wait time. The mathematical foundation under most WFM scheduling tools.

AHT — Average Handle TimeREFERENCE
Core Metric

Talk time plus hold time plus after-call work, averaged across interactions. The primary input into staffing/capacity calculations alongside volume.

ASA — Average Speed of AnswerREFERENCE
Core Metric

The average time a contact waits before being answered. Usually tracked alongside service level as a customer-experience proxy.

OccupancyREFERENCE
Utilization Metric

The percentage of logged-in time an agent spends actively handling contacts (talk + after-call work) versus waiting for the next one. High occupancy sustained too long is a leading indicator of burnout.

ShrinkageREFERENCE
Capacity Planning

The percentage of paid time an agent is not available to handle contacts — breaks, training, meetings, PTO, absenteeism. Applied on top of Erlang C staffing math to get the real headcount needed.

AdherenceREFERENCE
Real-Time Management

How closely an agent's actual activity matches their published schedule, usually expressed as a percentage. The core metric real-time WFM teams monitor intraday.

FTE — Full-Time EquivalentREFERENCE
Headcount Planning

A standardized unit of headcount (typically one person working full-time hours) used to compare and budget staffing levels across part-time, full-time, and blended schedules.

Service LevelREFERENCE
Core Metric

The percentage of contacts answered within a target time threshold (e.g., "80% of calls answered in 20 seconds," written as 80/20). The most common contact center SLA metric, and the target Erlang C staffing math solves for.

Forecast Accuracy (MAPE)REFERENCE
Forecasting

Mean Absolute Percentage Error — the standard way WFM teams measure how far actual volume/AHT deviated from the forecast. Lower MAPE means tighter, more trustworthy staffing plans.

WFO — Workforce OptimizationREFERENCE
Category Term

The broader vendor category that bundles WFM (forecasting/scheduling) with quality management, analytics, and coaching — what NICE, Verint, and Calabrio sell as unified suites.
